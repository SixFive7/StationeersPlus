# Power Grid Plus -- RESEARCH

Project-scoped internals: how the mod is built, every patch site and what it does, the design decisions behind the choices, and the game-internals knowledge the mod relies on. Forward-looking work and pending decisions live in `TODO.md`, not here.

The mod is a derivative of Sukasa's [Re-Volt](https://github.com/sukasa/revolt) (MIT, Copyright (c) 2025 Sukasa) trimmed to a pure-patch subset, plus original features on top. A read-only clone of the upstream lives at `.work/revolt-source/`.

---

## 1. Overview

Power Grid Plus replaces the cable-network power simulation outright and adds a three-tier transmission-voltage policy on top of the vanilla cable types. One Harmony prefix owns `ElectricityManager.ElectricityTick` and returns false; the vanilla per-network `PowerTick` trio (`Initialise` / `CalculateState` / `ApplyState`) is never called and there is no vanilla fallback path. Each tick, all on the power worker thread, the mod builds one grid snapshot (topology plus a single boundary read of every device's demand, output, and control state), detects structural faults against it (wrong-tier cable burns, cycle faults, producer isolation), runs a global allocator that solves every network at once (rigid demand, storage charge from surplus, storage discharge into shortfall, priority-ordered segmenter dispatch, leaf-first shedding), applies the converged result in a mod-owned write-back (net readouts, fuse and cable protection, storage settlement, consumer work-accumulator drains), and reconciles Powered flags, vanilla ledgers, and its own audits in the tick tail. A network is either fully served or cleanly dark with a visible fault; vanilla's partial-power brownouts, mid-tick races, and billing leaks are gone by construction.

The device-fix half (transformer / battery / APC exploit and leak fixes) is inherited from Re-Volt; the atomic snapshot pipeline, the allocator with its three flow classes (rigid demand, soft storage charge, elastic storage discharge), the fault system with its four 60-second lockouts, and the segment adapter layer are original. The voltage-tier half (`normal`, `heavy`, `superHeavy` cables as three mutually exclusive voltages, bridged only by transformers / APCs; per-device tier rules; reactive cable-burn enforcement; burn-reason tooltips on wreckages) is original. Two further original additions round out the mod: a writable per-device `LogicPassthroughMode` that makes power-network bridging devices (transformers, stationary batteries, linked power transmitter / receiver dishes, APCs, and docked rocket power umbilical pairs) logic-transparent, and a deterministic `CableNetwork` / `StructureNetwork` merge fix that keeps network `ReferenceId`s identical across multiplayer peers.

It is a **pure-patch mod**: no asset bundle, no new prefabs, no recipes for new items. The only added content is a `GameData/cable-recipes.xml` overlay that doubles the super-heavy cable coil's crafting cost. Removing the mod from a save reverts behaviour to vanilla: cable Watts caps are enforced at runtime only (`Cable.MaxVoltage` in the save is never written), fault lockouts are transient, and the three side-car XMLs the mod writes into the save ZIP (per-device `LogicPassthroughMode`, per-transformer Priority, per-wreckage burn reasons) are skipped silently by the vanilla loader.

## 3. Architecture

> **Revision history for this section.** Two retired architectures precede this one: the Re-Volt-derived `PowerGridTick` subclass (deleted in the 2026-06-10 rework), and the vanilla-trio-inside-atomic-phases design (2026-06-10 through 2026-07-12: the unmodified vanilla `PowerTick` ran inside OBSERVE / ENFORCE phases and every behaviour was delivered through device-method patches plus the global allocator, extended on 2026-07-02 with the unified flow classes, the segment adapter contract, and the ledger adoption). On 2026-07-12 the mod was rebuilt around a mod-owned data plane (POWER.md section 0 decisions 22-26, the B + D1 redesign): one grid snapshot with a single boundary read, a mod-owned write-back, and no vanilla `PowerTick` calls at all; the device-method power patches survive as presentation shims. Section 8 carries the dated decision log. The repo-root `POWER.md` is the authoritative algorithm spec end to end; this section is its prose companion with file-level anchors.

### 3.1 Plugin bootstrap

The plugin is a `BaseUnityPlugin` (`PowerGridPlus.Plugin`) whose `Awake` initialises the shared `MainThreadDispatcher` (linked from `Patterns/Threading/`), binds the BepInEx config, and registers `Prefab.OnPrefabsLoaded` as the deferred patch site. Inside that callback the plugin first refuses to load alongside an incompatible mod (`TryFindIncompatibleMod` matches loaded-assembly names: Re-Volt rewrites the same power tick, MoreCables adds cable tiers the voltage-tier policy cannot classify), then sets `MOD.Networking.Required = true`, registers the three LaunchPadBooster network messages (`PassthroughModeMessage`, `PriorityMessage`, `FaultRegistrySnapshotMessage`) and installs itself as the `IJoinSuffixSerializer`, runs `Harmony.PatchAll()`, subscribes `CableCostPatches.ApplyRecipeCost` to `WorldManager.OnGameDataLoaded` (the runtime recipe override must run after every mod's GameData overlay has been processed, or this mod's own overlay clobbers the configured value), and calls `Ic10ConstantsPatcher.Apply()` (extends `ProgrammableChip.AllConstants` and the syntax-highlighting `ScriptEnum` arrays). The whole block is wrapped in try/catch -- a failure logs fatal but does not crash the game.

### 3.2 One owner, no vanilla fallback

Vanilla computes wired power per network: each `CableNetwork` settles independently inside its own `PowerTick` trio, coupled to its neighbours only by a one-tick-lagged `PotentialLoad` mirror, with demand read live (and torn) mid-solve, delivery ratio-scaled into partial power, and billing routed through the never-zeroed `_powerProvided` ledger. Every earlier PowerGridPlus architecture kept that machinery running and patched around it, which left two authorities computing the same watts and a standing class of gaps at their seams: presentation without a carrier (the Luna deadlock: 50 kW of granted battery charge behind transformers advertised 0 on both terminals and idled forever, fault-free), one-tick-lagged bills browning out source networks, demand tear un-powering a fabricator on its own print-start spike, and ledger residue turning into free energy.

The 2026-07-12 rebuild removes the second authority instead of patching the seams. `AtomicElectricityTickPatch` owns the whole tick; the vanilla trio never runs; the mod builds its own model of the grid (SNAPSHOT), solves it (PROTECT / ALLOCATE), applies the result itself (WRITE-BACK plus the tick tail), and then runs the two vanilla phase copies (device tick, IC10). The patched `GetUsedPower` / `GetGeneratedPower` surfaces on segmenters and storage devices no longer deliver anything: they are presentation shims serving the allocator's published totals to tooltips, hovers, IC10, third-party callers, and the next tick's boundary read. Everything the mod builds itself is race-free by construction (POWER.md section 0 decision 25): single-writer thread ownership, phase sequencing, or a synchronization design proven to conserve under every interleaving; "a smaller race than vanilla" is not an accepted end state.

### 3.3 The atomic tick

The atomic tick (`Patches/AtomicElectricityTickPatch.cs`) owns `ElectricityManager.ElectricityTick` via prefix-return-false (host-only, `GameManager.RunSimulation` gate). Vanilla's per-network `PowerTick` trio (`Initialise` / `CalculateState` / `ApplyState`) is NEVER called; the mod owns the whole data plane (POWER.md section 0 decisions 24-26, the B + D1 redesign). Phases per tick:

1. **HOUSEKEEPING**: the world-load boundary drain (`Core/LoadBoundary.DrainPending`: the deferred load clears run here on the worker, never on the load path racing an in-flight tick), the accumulator-debit reconcile (`Core/MainThreadDebitQueue.Reconcile`), the one-shot unknown-bridge census (3.11) and ledger-adoption sweep (3.9) plus the ledger tick-boundary audit, the save/load self-check, registry hygiene, and the emergency-light toggle drain. The one-shots are armed at plugin load and re-armed by `FaultRegistryLoadPatches` on every world load, so a stale saved ledger can never bill through the first tick.
2. **SNAPSHOT**: `Core/GridSnapshot.Build`: topology from `lock(DeviceList)` plus the vanilla power-port membership predicate (`device.PowerCables[i].CableNetwork == net`; the unlocked `PowerDeviceList` lazy getter is never touched), plus the single boundary read: one `GetUsedPower` / `GetGeneratedPower` sample per device per tick, with the accumulator consumer classes reconstructed via `Core/DemandModel`. The rows also carry per-net Required / Potential sums, the weakest cable cap, the lowest-rated fuse, and the sorted segmenter roster.
3. **PROTECT**: server-side OFF-as-reset sweep (`OffAsResetSweep.cs`), wrong-tier cable burns (`VoltageTierEnforcer.Run(snap)`: the worker path detects from the snapshot rows, the main-thread topology-event recheck gates power flow on the write-back-published net `PotentialLoad` / `RequiredLoad`, and the tier cache shared by both paths is a `ConcurrentDictionary`), CYCLE_FAULT detection (`CycleGraphBuilder.FindCycleFaultedSegmenters(snap)`, directed-SCC), and producer-isolation VARIABLE_VOLTAGE_FAULT (`VariableVoltageFaultDetector.Run(tick, snap)` + `ProducerClassifier.cs`, with a cable-burn fallback for unclassified producer-like devices). Newly locked producers are zeroed IN the snapshot (`snap.ZeroFaultedProducers`); the old OBSERVE / re-observe pair collapsed into that in-place zero, so the allocator solves the corrected grid with no second observation pass.
4. **ALLOCATE**: `PowerAllocator.RunAtomic` (3.4); GATHER consumes the snapshot rows. Then the per-tick full fault snapshots to clients (`FaultRegistrySnapshotMessage.cs`).
5. **WRITE-BACK**: `Core/WriteBack.Run` applies the allocator's plan in one pass: the net HUD / MP / logic load fields (`RequiredLoad` / `CurrentLoad` / `PotentialLoad` / `ShortfallLoad`), the deterministic lowest-rated fuse blow (vanilla picked a random breakable fuse), the 20-tick generator-overflow cable burn (ported verbatim from the retired `GetBreakableCables` replacement), storage settlement with credit == grant by construction (the battery charge-cost divisor + the sub-500 W trickle floor applied here), the plain-consumer delivery shim (POWER.md section 0 decision 28: the classified delivery-effect rows receive `device.ReceivePower(net, granted demand)` on verdict-LIVE nets only, per-device try/catch with a 600-tick warning throttle; a DEAD net delivers nothing, vanilla's empty-providers equivalence), and the consumer accumulator drains per decision 26 (main-thread queue for the main-clock classes, worker-direct for the rest; a DEAD net drains nothing, so debts freeze exactly and bill in full on revival).
6. **Tail**: `PoweredPresentation.ReconcileEnforceTail()` asserts BOTH Powered edges on routed segmenters from the health verdict (3.8), `PoweredOwnership.SweepEnforceTail(tick, snap)` iterates the snapshot rows and owns every plain device's Powered edges (3.13), `LedgerAdoption.SettleEnforceTail()` settles every owned segmenter ledger (3.9), then `PoweredSetConformance` and the charge / discharge delivery audits (3.11). Runs before the device tick, so the IC10 LOGIC phase reads the settled ledger (transformer `PowerActual` equals actual current throughput).
7. **DEVICE + LOGIC**: vanilla copies (`AllPoweredThings.OnPowerTick`, then `CircuitHolders.Execute`); third-party device-tick patches still fire.

### 3.4 The allocator: one demand vector, three flow classes

`PowerAllocator.RunAtomic` builds its per-tick model from `GridSnapshot.Current` (GATHER: per-net rigid demand, generator supply, and the sorted segmenter roster come straight from the snapshot rows; the contributor / elastic / soft rosters via the adapters; no live device read happens here), computes a true topological order over conducting contributor edges (ORDER, Kahn; cycle members are already locked so the live graph is a DAG), iterates a backward-desire / forward-supply fixed point with re-decidable shed and grow-only overload (DECIDE, max `2N+4` rounds), computes net-local elastic shares, publishes the presentation caches and snapshots plus the write-back plan, and audits itself (conservation check). Every ordering is integer-keyed (depth, priority, ReferenceId; float claims quantised to whole Watts) for multiplayer determinism.

After convergence, the lockout commits, and the shortfall census (all reading the deciding state), ticks that shed anything run a stranded-inflow clawback (`RunAtomic`, between the census and the elastic-share pass): every network whose total committed inflow (rigid plus soft) exceeds its total consumption (served demand, granted pulls, charge, and soft pulls; soft counts because the soft stage funds charge from the rigid firm residual) gives the surplus back from its active suppliers in reverse grant order, leaf to source, shrinking throughput and pull together so the per-seg invariant holds. This closes the stranded-inflow bug (a shed-trip tick where upstream partially funded a claim the forward pass then shed left inflow billed upstream with zero outflow; first seen as the net 487688 conservation warning, delta 12.27 W, and reproduced on four tower nets in one boot). The strand is measured over the full checker pool; a rigid-only formula clawed charge funding on a chargers' input net (net 625036, delta -26.67 W) before this was corrected. Nothing is re-split or re-granted, so allocation off the stranded chains is bit-identical to the deciding passes and decisions never move. Shedding is leaf-first: inside `ForwardSupplyAndShed` a seg whose OutNet still feeds ACTIVE child segs is protected exactly like a step-up (`FeedsActiveSeg`), so sheds escalate leaf-to-trunk and a no-load trunk never sheds out from under its subtree. Shed victim selection is `SelectShedVictims` (tier-major best-fit-decreasing, POWER.md section 8.3.3): lowest priority tier first, and within a tier the smallest whole-Watt claim that covers the remaining deficit alone (tie: lowest ReferenceId), else the largest claim (tie: lowest ReferenceId), repeated until the tier spills into the next. The selector is a pure static function of (candidates, deficit), with claims floored to whole Watts and the deficit rounded up past the Eps tolerance so quantisation can never under-shed into a spurious elastic hit-max; ScenarioRunner's `pgp-shed-victim-fixture` scenario drives it directly with synthetic candidate sets. It replaced the flat (priority ASC, ReferenceId ASC) walk after a controlled A/B showed cross-boot trajectory comparison could never validate a selection change (boot shed races draw different victims per run); the synthetic fixtures are the purpose-built evidence that decision called for.

Three flow classes ride the same sweep:

- **Rigid** is ordinary consumer demand plus the contributor pulls that serve it, and it alone drives every fault decision (shed, structural overload, supply overload). The backward pass splits each network's residual rigid need (demand minus generators) over its suppliers priority-tier-first and EffCap-proportional within a tier, capped per contributor at its effective capacity; `DesiredPull = DesiredThroughput * max(m, 1) + quiescent`. The forward pass grants in topo order and re-decides shedding against settled upstream supply each round.
- **Soft** is storage charge (battery, APC cell, umbilical cell). It propagates leaf-to-source in the SAME backward walk with the same tier-first proportional splitter, but each contributor's soft capacity is `EffCap - rigid desired throughput`: soft never displaces rigid. Forward, soft is granted per network from a three-rung funding ladder consumed strictly in order (POWER.md section 9.2): the local firm residual (generators plus contributor inflow left after rigid loads and granted rigid pulls), the soft inflow arriving through the net's suppliers, and LAST the net's elastic leftover (the eligible discharge capacity the rigid settlement did not consume), proportionally over local charge requests and consumer soft pulls via one ratio, capped by the weakest cable's remaining headroom. Elastic funds soft ONLY from that per-net leftover after the full rigid settlement, which is what makes battery-to-battery transfer possible; recipients stay priority-tiered and physics-capped, and a sustained transfer loop would need a directed cable cycle, which the PROTECT-phase cycle DFS already locks (battery / APC / umbilical edges participate in that walk, direction input to output). Unmet soft desire is silently clamped: never a shed, never an overload, never a lockout, never a dead-input cue. The proportional split kills the deleted surplus walk's double-count by construction (parallel contributors divide a downstream request instead of each propagating it whole). A store's grant also requires a BILLABLE owner: GATHER refuses enrollment while the owner sits in a fault lockout (a locked-out owner's published bill is zero on every surface, so a granted share could never be billed or delivered; store 464386's cell was granted 22 to 1000 W for exactly one 120-tick dawn lockout with zero credits before this gate existed), the per-round `SoftOwnerBillable` gate covers an owner that sheds or overloads inside the deciding loop (the billable-desire sums also stop upstream soft inflow from being sized for it), and an inoperable battery (vanilla `IsOperable` false) is never enrolled.
- **Elastic** is storage discharge. Its CAPACITY stays net-local: it covers the rigid shortfall left after generators and contributor inflow on its own output network first, split proportionally against effective discharge caps, and then donates its leftover to the same net's soft pool (the lowest funding rung above); the soft flow that donation funds rides the normal soft class and can cross contributors. Each elastic's published share is `rigidShare + softTopUp` (top-up distributed full-or-proportional to leftover, never above `EffDischarge`), so the donor's `GetGeneratedPower` advertise covers the transfer and vanilla drains exactly what was granted. Locked or overloaded elastics donate nothing, exactly as they supply nothing.

The contributor's quiescent draw is carried exactly once: on the rigid pull whenever the contributor carries any rigid flow, else on the soft pull. In the forward pass the soft pull carries only the remainder the GRANTED rigid pull left uncovered (`max(0, quiescent - granted rigid pull)`), so the per-seg invariant stays exact even when a quiescent-bearing pull is granted partially on a short net. Because soft rides the same priority tiers as rigid, a high-priority transformer's storage charges before a low-priority one's (a documented behaviour change vs the priority-blind surplus walk).

### 3.5 Per-segment presentation totals

The publish tail computes, for every routed contributor, `TotalThrough = Throughput + SoftThrough` (output-side delivery) and `TotalPull = TotalThrough * max(m, 1) + quiescent` (input-side bill, exact by construction whenever the seg conducts), and writes `TransformerSupplyCache.Set(RefId, TotalThrough, TotalPull)` for EVERY routed seg kind: Transformer, wireless pair, and APC alike. The APC additionally publishes `ApcPassthroughCache = TotalThrough` (its fresh input-side passthrough bill), `ApcCellDischargeCache` (the cell-only share, so `DischargeSpeed` means the cell rate), and `SoftSupplyShareCache = TotalThrough + cellShare` (vanilla `GetGeneratedPower` bundles passthrough and cell). Per-device storage charge shares go to `SoftDemandShareCache`; the storage patches min-clamp their advertised demand against them, and the write-back credits each store exactly its grant. Inactive contributors (shed / overloaded / cycle-faulted) carry all-zero totals. Publishing totals for every kind is what guarantees a granted soft flow has a carrier on both terminals of its segment: a battery charging behind a transformer or wireless pair sees the charge advertised downstream AND billed upstream in the same tick.

One user-visible consequence: network `PotentialLoad` / `RequiredLoad` mirrors, hover tooltips, and IC10 reads of transformer / wireless throughput now include storage-charge flow, not just running machines.

### 3.6 The segment adapter contract (`SegAdapters.cs`)

GATHER no longer open-codes each bridge class; it consults one `ISegAdapter` per modelled class. An adapter answers `Describes(device)` (pure type membership) and `TryDescribe(device, out SegSpec)` (the device's per-tick PHYSICAL description: flow kind, terminal networks, capacities, distance multiplier, quiescent draw). GATHER attaches allocator POLICY on top (priority, lockout state, shed bookkeeping). Two flow models:

- **Routed**: power crosses the device within the tick it is granted; the device becomes one allocator Seg. The output side delivers `TotalThrough`; the input side bills `TotalThrough * max(m, 1) + quiescent` the same tick.
- **Buffered**: power stops in an internal cell; nothing crosses within the tick. The device contributes storage roster entries instead of a Seg (cell charge = Soft demander, cell discharge = Elastic supplier); the physical crossing happens outside the allocator.

### 3.7 PowerTransmitterPlus interop tiers and the billing ownership handshake

`PowerTransmitterPlusInterop.cs` resolves once at first use (no per-tick reflection; PowerGridPlus never hard-references PowerTransmitterPlus) and degrades through three tiers:

1. **ModApi** (PowerTransmitterPlus 1.9.0+, `PowerTransmitterPlus.ModApi` with `Version >= 1`): binds `EffectiveMaxCapacity`, `TryGetLink` (returns false when unlinked, killing the stale-distance leak), `SourceDrawMultiplier`, and the `GetTransferDebt` / `SetTransferDebt` ledger accessors (bound leniently), then calls `ClaimBillingOwnership("net.powergridplus")` exactly once. While the claim is held, PowerTransmitterPlus's native wireless debt billing (the `UsePower` debt inflation, the `GetUsedPower` cap lift, and the standalone debt ceiling) stands down and this mod's allocator is the single billing authority; the capacity advertise, receiver drain-cap lift, beam visuals, and link handling stay active on the PowerTransmitterPlus side. The claim is per-call-checked over there, so a late claim is safe; a rejected claim (another owner) leaves the read surface bound and falls back to delivery-gate containment.
2. **Legacy** (the shipped Workshop 1.8.0 line): `DistanceCostShared.SourceDrawMultiplier` (public wrapper, added after the 1.8.0 tag) or the internal `GetMultiplier` (the identical computation; the shipped build has only this), plus `MaxCapacityConfigSync.GetEffectiveMaxCapacity` and the vanilla `_linkedReceiverDistance` field. No ownership handshake exists at this tier; the `DeliveryGatePatch` + fresh-pull prefixes keep the ledgers bounded instead, exactly as before the ModApi existed.
3. **Absent**: `m = 1` and the vanilla link rating (`MaxPowerTransmission` minus the distance-delivery loss).

Exactly one Info line states the resolved tier and, on the ModApi tier, the claim outcome. The tiers agree by construction: the ModApi forwards to the same internals the legacy tier binds, so switching tiers never changes the allocator's numbers for the same world state. The source-draw multiplier `m = 1 + k * distance_km` (k host-synced, default 5) inflates the transmitter's input-side draw; delivery is never derated for distance under PowerTransmitterPlus.

### 3.8 Powered presentation policy (routed segmenters)

Vanilla `PowerTick.ApplyState` derived a device's Powered flag from what it consumed this tick, so a healthy segmenter idling at zero throughput billed a fresh pull of 0 and vanilla flipped it to Powered=False: players read "device broken" on a charger whose batteries are simply full. Policy: HEALTHY = enrolled in this tick's roster, carrying no fault, and either conducting (`TotalThrough > 0`) or idle on an input network that has effective supply and no unmet rigid demand. ALLOCATE publishes the healthy set and the enrolled-seg roster by volatile reference swap (`PoweredPresentation.cs`). With ApplyState retired nothing else writes a routed segmenter's Powered flag any more, so the tick tail owns BOTH edges outright: `ReconcileEnforceTail` asserts Powered from the health verdict on the anchor AND the wireless partner alike (healthy = true, unhealthy = false) via the vanilla self-marshaling `SetPowerFromThread`; edges fire only on an actual transition, zero steady-state churn. A pair publishes health under both halves' ReferenceIds. An idle segmenter on a DARK input (night-time solar feed) is deliberately NOT healthy: the tail un-powers it, matching the dead-input hover cue. Server-authoritative by construction: the tail only runs in the host's atomic tick, and `SetPower` routes through the normal interactable replication; on a client the published set stays empty.

### 3.9 Ledger adoption (`_powerProvided`)

The private `_powerProvided` on Transformer / AreaPowerControl / PowerTransmitter / PowerReceiver is vanilla's deferred billing handshake (UsePower charges it output-side, GetUsedPower bills it, ReceivePower repays it). The game never zeroes it; at 0.2.6403 it is not serialized into saves (no SaveData member carries it, per the 0.2.6403 write-site census), so a nonzero value is runtime accumulation within the session, residue from an older-version save, or an external writer. Under the pre-redesign fresh-pull billing it drifted structurally: a conducting wireless transmitter was drained roughly `TotalThrough` on the wireless network but repaid the full `TotalPull = TotalThrough * m + quiescent` on the source network, `-((m - 1) * TotalThrough + quiescent)` per conducting tick, compounding into giant negative credits over a session (observed: -176,226 on Luna transmitter 464520) that become free energy the moment vanilla billing reads the ledger again (`min(cap, debt)` bills 0 until the credit burns off). Since the B + D1 redesign nothing writes the ledger in-tick at all (the vanilla UsePower / ReceivePower traffic ran inside the retired ApplyState), but saved residue and external writers remain possible, and surfaces still READ the ledger (`PowerActual`, the receiver relay debt). Two counter-measures in `LedgerAdoption.cs`, both host-only inside the atomic tick:

- **World-load sweep**: on the first atomic tick after a load (armed at plugin load, re-armed by `FaultRegistryLoadPatches`), zero the ledger on every modelled segmenter, both signs, NaN included, BEFORE the first boundary read can serve a stale value through a vanilla `GetUsedPower` fallback. One Info line per zeroed device plus a summary.
- **Per-tick tail settle**: at the tick tail (after the write-back), negatives clamp to zero (silent counter; that is the free-energy metric) and each OWNED ledger settles to its vanilla-equivalent standing value: Transformer := `TotalThrough` (which also makes IC10 `PowerActual` read actual current throughput), transmitter := 0 (its bill was paid in full this tick), receiver := `min(debt, TotalThrough)` (preserves the live relay debt that drives the wireless drain and the beam visuals, shears only stuck overshoot). Writes inside a 0.01 W tolerance are skipped. The earlier `[0, 2 * TotalPull]` upper-bound rewrite is gone: under fresh-pull billing vanilla loses its self-draining `min(cap, ledger)` force, residue strands instead of draining, and healthy steady values legitimately exceeded that bound. An exact ledger audit (always-on, no config entry) replaces the earlier approximate high-water detector. Because the settle pins every owned ledger to a known value at the tick tail and nothing legitimate writes `_powerProvided` between settles at all (the vanilla write sites lived inside the retired ApplyState), ownership violations are detected exactly, in three classes: a tick-boundary check (the field must equal the recorded post-settle value exactly at the next tick's start), a settle-tail identity (the pre-settle field must equal the boundary value within 0.01 W), and a non-finite backstop (NaN/Infinity, repaired by the settle). The per-mutation bracket wrappers around the six vanilla write sites are deleted with those write sites' caller. Counts are exact; the boundary check emits one aggregated warning per 600 ticks while new anomalies arrive, with totals per class and the worst offender. Zero anomalies means zero log lines; any anomaly means something outside PowerGridPlus wrote a ledger it owns.

The AreaPowerControl is swept at load but deliberately NOT settled per tick. Under vanilla, `APC.UsePower` drained the internal cell against a POSITIVE ledger one tick after the cell covered an output shortfall; with the trio retired the write-back drains the cell directly by its granted share, the vanilla handshake never runs inside the tick, and the APC ledger is simply left alone between load sweeps. Wireless halves route through the ModApi debt accessors when resolved, else through cached `FieldInfo` on the vanilla field.

### 3.10 Conservation checker

`ConservationChecker.cs`, always on (no config entry since the 2026-07-13 settings rework). Two invariants over the allocator's own converged grants, audited at the end of ALLOCATE, so a violation is by definition a PowerGridPlus bug and never a player problem: per network, granted inflow (rigid seg inflow + soft inflow + granted elastic + the implied generator draw, derived as the residual clamped to `[0, genSupply]` so both failure directions surface) must equal granted outflow (rigid served + storage charge + rigid pulls + soft pulls) within 0.5 W; per contributor seg, `TotalPull == TotalThrough * max(m, 1) + quiescent` whenever the seg conducts (checked one-sided against the quiescent when it does not). Warnings are throttled to once per network / per seg per 600 ticks (about 5 minutes at the 2 Hz power tick).

### 3.11 Shortfall diagnostics, the unknown-bridge census, and the delivery audits

`ShortfallDiagnostics.cs` labels every allocator network's end-of-tick RIGID state and publishes one immutable snapshot per tick by volatile swap: **Served** (0, no unmet rigid demand), **Dry** (1, unmet with every remaining feed genuinely exhausted: source-side shortage, honest darkness), **Throttled** (2, unmet with a deliberately closed valve: a locked / shed / overloaded supplier, a zero-effective-capacity `Setting = 0` firewall, or a locked elastic), and **Deadlock** (3, unmet while the allocator's own accounting says supply existed: an active supplier had headroom above 0.5 W AND its input network retained undelivered supply). Deadlock is the invisible-deadlock regression shape and must be zero on a healthy build; it is checked before the throttle rung so a closed valve on the same net cannot mask a routing failure. Diagnostics only (nothing in the mod reads it back); the ScenarioRunner `pgp-rearch-suite` census joins the snapshot via reflection, and the byte values are a cross-assembly contract.

`UnknownBridgeCensus.cs`: on the first atomic tick after a world load, every scene device that is an `ElectricalInputOutput` subclass but is neither in `SegmentingDeviceRegistry`'s known set nor described by any adapter is reported, one Info line per TYPE. Reporting is the only handling: an unknown bridge keeps its vanilla power methods unpatched and the snapshot's boundary read samples its `GetUsedPower` / `GetGeneratedPower` as plain rigid demand / generation on each side, the conservative fallback. The census makes the gap visible instead of silent when a third-party mod ships its own two-port power device.

`ChargeDeliveryAudit.cs` is the grant-vs-credit seam detector (always-on, no config entry, the ledger-audit posture), closing the last unaudited leg: the allocator's grants are audited by the conservation checker and the vanilla-facing bills by the ledger audit, but what actually LANDS in a store was not, and a settlement-side adjustment can silently under- or over-credit with every other invariant green (the APC quiescent-subtraction wrinkle that motivated it). ALLOCATE publishes a per-tick charge-grant snapshot (refId, granted watts, charge-side net, store kind, straight from the softs roster); the delivery side records each store's credited watts at the write-back settlement site (`Core/WriteBack`), never as a `PowerStored` field diff (the store fields are float32, so a 230 MJ nuclear bank quantizes at 16 J per ulp and genuine trickle credits read as multiples of 16). Since the redesign the audit verifies plan-vs-settlement identity (credit == grant by construction; the old vanilla-write observation brackets had nothing left to bracket), so it exists to catch a future settlement-path change that breaks the identity. The tick tail compares credited against granted per store, gated on the charge-side net classifying Served and on the battery band `[granted / BatteryChargeEfficiency, granted]` (the configured charge cost is legitimate; the sub-500 W trickle floor stores the full delivery). Tolerance 0.5 W, the ConservationChecker basis. A store whose charge gate legitimately closed between grant and settlement (the APC cell's Mode-based `IsCharged` fill edge) is marked moot for the tick by the APC billing patch (`MarkChargeGateClosed`) rather than tolerated numerically, and a store owned by an unbillable contributor is never granted at all (section 3.4), so the fault-lockout class is impossible by construction. The ScenarioRunner `pgp-rearch-suite` drives `IsViolation` with six synthetic fixture cases (`RSD P<n>` lines) and judges the live window through its `delivery=` verdict field.

The 2026-07-08 auditor round added five more always-on surfaces, all in the ledger-audit posture (exact counters, one aggregated warning per 600 ticks, zero lines healthy, world-load clear), folded into the suite's single `audits=` verdict field: `DischargeDeliveryAudit.cs` (the charge audit's second direction: drains recorded at the write-back settlement site for battery and both umbilical halves, compared at the tick tail against the granted elastic share `rigidShare + softTopUp`, Served-gated, exact band; the APC cell is out of scope by design, the allocator publishes no APC-cell grants to it and the write-back drains the cell directly; charge / discharge same-tick disambiguation is by settlement kind by construction); `TickDurationWatchdog.cs` (Stopwatch spans around the atomic tick and `RunAtomic`, threshold `min(max(8 * rolling median, 50 ms), 400 ms)` from a 256-tick ring recomputed every 64 ticks; the warning fires only when the allocator is the dominant cause of the overrun, its span's excess over its own rolling median explaining at least half the amount the tick ran over, so environmental stalls, autosave serialization, GC, OS scheduling, that slow a tick while the allocator ran its normal few ms stay silent, which is what the gate-14 soak's two false positives exposed; zero per-tick allocation); `PoweredSetConformance.cs` (tick-tail assert that healthy roster members read Powered true past a one-tick marshal grace); `RegistryHygiene.cs` (600-tick sweep pruning expired and destroyed-device entries from the four fault registries; `Thing.Find` dictionary lookup plus reference-test null check, worker-safe); and `SaveLoadSelfCheck.cs` (one-shot post-load: registries empty, priority sidecar restored equals loaded, ledger sweep ran). The same round's solar / wind output latches (and the consumer demand latches before them) are gone: the snapshot's single boundary read makes every device's demand and output tick-stable by construction, so there is nothing left to latch.

## 6. Multiplayer protocol

The mod sets `MOD.Networking.Required = true` (LaunchPadBooster). The connection handshake checks the mod version; clients without a matching DLL are rejected at join time.

The mod registers three LaunchPadBooster `INetworkMessage` types (`PassthroughModeMessage`, `PriorityMessage`, `FaultRegistrySnapshotMessage`) and an `IJoinSuffixSerializer` whose suffix carries, in order: the explicit per-device `LogicPassthroughMode` overrides, the six per-kind Passthrough Default settings (fixed order: small transformer, other transformer, battery, APC, power transmitter, umbilical), the per-transformer Priority overrides, and a remaining-ticks snapshot of all four fault registries (violator names included for the variable-voltage entries), so a joining client lands mid-lockout with correct flash and countdown state before the first per-tick snapshot arrives. The suffix no longer carries shed or overload toggles; those behaviors are always on and need no sync. The rest of the mod's state reaches clients through vanilla state-sync:

- The power tick runs on the host; clients see device `Powered` states via the normal `Device.SetPower` replication. That includes the Powered presentation reconcile and the ownership sweep: both only ever run inside the host's atomic tick, `SetPowerFromThread` routes the write through the normal interactable path, and on a client peer the published sets stay empty, so clients simply mirror the host's replicated flag. The one client-side exception is `Battery.Powered`: vanilla's overridden getter reads a host-only field, so `BatteryPoweredMirrorPatches` reads the replicated `InteractPowered.State` instead on clients.
- Client HUD net loads ride the same vanilla delta serialization as always (`RequiredLoad` / `CurrentLoad` / `PotentialLoad` / `ShortfallLoad` on each `CableNetwork`); the write-back fills the fields host-side each tick (`Core/WriteBack`), so the network analyser cartridge, hover readouts, and IC10 network reads on a client show the allocator's converged figures with no extra messages.
- Fault state reaches clients as per-tick FULL snapshots (`FaultRegistrySnapshotMessage`, one per registry kind: shed, overload, cycle, variable voltage, dead-input). A non-empty registry broadcasts its complete `(refId, remainingTicks)` map every tick; an empty registry sends exactly one empty snapshot on the non-empty-to-empty transition, then goes silent. The client converts remaining ticks to a `MonotonicClock` expiry on receive (the tick counter does not advance on a non-simulating peer), snapshots self-heal after packet loss, and the join-suffix handshake seeds a joining client's mirrors before the first snapshot.
- Per-transformer Priority replicates via `PriorityMessage` plus the join suffix, and persists host-side in its save side-car.
- `LogicPassthroughMode` per-device mode (stored for `Transformer`, `Battery`, `AreaPowerControl`, `PowerTransmitter`, `PowerReceiver`, and the rocket umbilical halves): host-side in `PassthroughModeStore`, persisted in the save-ZIP side-car. The `SetLogicValue` prefixes are server-only (`NetworkManager.IsServer`), so a client tablet write routes through the host. On a server-side change the host broadcasts `PassthroughModeMessage` (`(ReferenceId, mode)`); each client applies it to its local store, dirties the device's networks, and refreshes. A joining client receives the full set of explicit overrides via `IJoinSuffixSerializer`. The data-device-list merge runs per-peer, so this synced mode is what lets a client compute the same merged membership the host does.
- The six per-kind Passthrough Default settings are server-authoritative, and the join suffix is their sole sync path: StationeersLaunchPad exposes no per-mod ConfigEntry UI while a save is loaded, so a mid-session host change cannot happen and no live-broadcast message exists. `PassthroughModeStore.GetDefaultMode` reads `PassthroughDefaultsSync.Effective*` (host value on a client, local config on host / single-player) for every device with no explicit mode entry, so a client whose local config differs from the host never computes a divergent data-device-list merge. Shedding and overload protection are always on and carry nothing on the wire.
- Wireless dish link symmetry: vanilla replicates `PowerTransmitter.LinkedReceiver` to clients (the `WirelessPower.Receiver` delta + join) but NOT `PowerReceiver.LinkedPowerTransmitter` (its setter sets no update flag; it is assigned only inside the `RunSimulation`-gated host-only `TryContactReceiver`). So on a client the dish bridge would be one-directional, because the bridge walk (`GetBridgeNetworks(rx)`, gated by `IsEnabledBridge`) reads the null back-reference. `DishLinkPatches` mirrors the back-reference on the client (idempotent, client-gated, with an MP-mutation audit) so the merge is symmetric. PowerTransmitterPlus neither sets nor relies on this back-reference on clients, so the two mods compose; a TODO in PowerTransmitterPlus records the option for it to own the mirror. See `Research/GameClasses/PowerTransmitter.md`.
- Device-list UI refresh: the merge mutates `DataDeviceList` silently (vanilla notifies no consumer). On a mode flip, a dish link change, an umbilical dock / undock, or a device built on a far bridged network, the refresh cascade (`CableNetworkPatches` + `DishLinkPatches` + `UmbilicalPassthroughPatches`, marshaled to the main thread by the shared dispatcher) re-fires `OnDeviceConnectToNetwork` so motherboard dropdowns / IC-housing / sensor caches rebuild. It runs per-peer (each peer computes its own merge), gated to whichever peer's state changed. See `Research/GameClasses/Motherboard.md`.
- The `CableNetwork` / `StructureNetwork` merge-determinism prefixes (`MergeDeterminismPatches`) exist purely for multiplayer correctness. Vanilla `Merge` keeps `list[0]` as the survivor, and server and client run the merge independently with no authoritative sync event; if their cable-graph iteration order differs, they keep different survivors and read different `CableNetwork.ReferenceId`s for the same cable indefinitely. Sorting the merge list by `ReferenceId` first makes the survivor a pure function of server-allocated ids, so every peer converges. See `Research/GameClasses/CableNetwork.md`.
- The burn-reason wreckage tooltip is computed server-side at the moment of `Cable.Break()`, held in the wreckage's `BurnReasonRegistry` attached state, and persisted across save / load via the `pwrgridplus-burnreason.xml` side-car. Clients see the wreckage Thing via normal replication; the tooltip postfix runs locally on each client, reading from the client's own `BurnReasonRegistry`. **A joining client therefore does not see burn reasons for wreckages that existed before the join** -- the reason is attached host-side and never replicated over the wire. Not currently considered a bug since the visual debris itself is the more important feedback; logged as a known limitation.

Side-car XMLs persist server-only state and are part of the save ZIP. A vanilla loader (no mod) ignores unknown entries silently, so saves remain backwards-compatible if the mod is removed.

## 7. Pitfalls and gotchas

- **`CableNetwork.DataDeviceList.get` checks the wrong flag.** Vanilla code reads `if (PowerDeviceListDirty) RefreshPowerAndDataDeviceLists()` instead of `DataDeviceListDirty`. This means a `DirtyDataDeviceList()` call alone does not invalidate `DataDeviceList`-reads until something else (cable add / remove, power-side dirty) flips the power flag too. In practice the per-tick power-side rebuild covers this, so the bug is silent. Anyone touching this code should not rely on `DataDeviceList.get` triggering a refresh after a data-only dirty. See `Research/GameClasses/CableNetwork.md` "Field shape and accessor quirk" for the verbatim accessor code.
- **`Cable.Break()` runs on worker threads.** The atomic tick runs on a UniTask worker. `BurnReasonRegistry._pendingByCell` is a `ConcurrentDictionary` precisely because worker code calls `RegisterPending` concurrently with main-thread reads. The matching `Attach` runs on the main thread in the `CableRuptured.OnRegistered` postfix (Unity Things are main-thread-only), so the registry's main-thread `ConditionalWeakTable` is fine. Adding new burn call sites needs to honour this split.
- **`Thing.GetPassiveTooltip` is a universal-base postfix.** The Burn-reason postfix patches the base method, not `CableRuptured`-specific (which does not override). Every tooltip in the game runs through this postfix; the `__instance is CableRuptured` filter is the first line. Sanity check before adding similar universal patches: is the work cheap enough to run on every hover?
- **APC patch deviation from Re-Volt 1.4.0.** Re-Volt declares its `UsePower`-bodied prefix as patching `GetUsedPower`; the parameter types only match `UsePower`. Power Grid Plus dropped the `UsePower` patch entirely (2026-05-28), and since the B + D1 redesign vanilla `UsePower` never runs inside the tick at all: the write-back drains the APC cell directly by its granted share. The mistargeted-prefix trap is moot here but still bites anyone porting other Re-Volt APC code.
- **`SaveHelper.Save`'s private 4-arg overload is the only sound patch target.** The public `Save(string, CancellationToken)` overload would `AmbiguousMatchException` at `PatchAll` time without the explicit `new[] { typeof(DirectoryInfo), typeof(string), typeof(bool), typeof(CancellationToken) }` argument-type array. See `PassthroughSaveLoadPatches.cs`.
- **`ElectronicsPrinter.RecipeComparable.GenerateRecipieList` is the canonical typo.** The vanilla method is mis-spelled (`Recipie`). Do not silently fix it in calls or the override will not bind.
- **Run-once Ic10ConstantsPatcher / LogicableInitializePatch composition.** Both are guarded by static flags. If PowerTransmitterPlus also runs (it follows the same pattern), the second mod's postfix sees the first mod's already-injected array and appends on top. Order is stable as long as both mods guard with their own `_applied` flag; never `Array.Copy` over a fresh `new T[vanillaLength]`.
- **Loaded saves with pre-existing mixed-tier networks trigger a cascade.** D11 retroactive enforcement burns the boundary cable of every pre-existing mixed-tier network on the first power-flowing tick after load. On a base that mixed tiers freely under vanilla rules this is destructive. The Option-C TODO entry proposes a default-off opt-in.
- **A `[HarmonyPatch]` on an inherited method the subclass does not override throws "Undefined target method" at `PatchAll` time and silently kills the rest of the batch.** The four `Logicable` logic-port methods (`CanLogicRead` / `CanLogicWrite` / `GetLogicValue` / `SetLogicValue`) are declared on base `Device`. `Battery`, `PowerTransmitter`, and `PowerReceiver` inherit them without overriding, so `[HarmonyPatch(typeof(Battery), nameof(Battery.SetLogicValue))]` compiles (`nameof` resolves through inheritance) but fails to resolve a *declared* method at patch time. The exception bails the whole `PatchAll` batch from that point on, disabling every patch class processed after it plus the post-`PatchAll` calls in `Plugin` (`CableCostPatches.ApplyRecipeCost`, `Ic10ConstantsPatcher.Apply`); which patches survive depends on HarmonyX's class-iteration order, so the failure looks intermittent. The fix is to patch the base type that declares the method (`Device`) and add a runtime `is`-type guard (`IsBridge`) for the subclasses you care about, which is why `InheritedLogicablePassthroughLogicPatches` targets `Device` while `TransformerPassthroughLogicPatches` patches `Transformer` directly (Transformer genuinely overrides the four methods, so a base-`Device` patch would be shadowed by its override). Full writeup: `Research/Patterns/HarmonyLogicableInheritedMethodTrap.md`.
- **A granted flow with no published carrier does not exist in-game.** The allocator can decide anything it likes; unless the decision lands in the caches that `GetGeneratedPower` / `GetUsedPower` serve, on BOTH terminals of the segment, the grid never sees it: the next tick's boundary read, the write-back's net fields, and every tooltip / IC10 surface all consume those caches. The 2026-07-02 Luna deadlock was exactly this: storage-charge grants were published only for APC segments, so 50 kW of granted battery charge behind transformers advertised 0 and billed 0, a permanent fault-free fixed point. Any new flow class or seg kind must publish its totals for every kind, and the conservation checker exists to catch the next such gap.
- **Vanilla's cross-network advertise is one tick lagged, so a fully dead island cannot bootstrap itself under vanilla relaxation.** A bridge advertises `min(cap, InputNetwork.PotentialLoad)`, where `PotentialLoad` is the LAST-completed-tick mirror. On a dead source network the mirror reads 0, so nothing downstream requests, so nothing flows, so the mirror stays 0. Real case: solar farms parked at zero efficiency whose tracker ICs are powered by the same dead farm nets can never restart without the pooled allocator (which sizes supply from fresh in-tick sums instead). Do not "simplify" a capacity read to the live `PotentialLoad`; the wireless pair's static link rating exists precisely because a live read collapsed to a cross-tick zero fixed point.
- **Vanilla ApplyState un-powered zero-demand devices.** Vanilla derived the Powered flag from what a device consumed this tick, with the only un-power path gated on `Device.AllowSetPower(net)`. With the trio retired that writer is gone entirely, which cuts both ways: an idle healthy charger can no longer be flagged broken, but nothing vanilla will ever move a Powered flag again either. The tick tail must therefore own BOTH edges for every device class it claims (`PoweredPresentation` reconcile for routed segmenters, `PoweredOwnership` sweep for plain devices, section 3.8 / 3.13); a device left out of both owners keeps its last flag forever.
- **The vanilla `_powerProvided` ledger is never zeroed by the game.** Under the pre-redesign fresh-pull billing it drifted structurally negative on wireless pairs (`-((m - 1) * TotalThrough + quiescent)` per conducting tick), and an accumulated credit becomes free energy for any code path that reads the ledger again. Since the B + D1 redesign nothing writes it in-tick at all, but at 0.2.6403 the field is not serialized into saves and older saves plus external writers can still carry residue, and readers remain (`PowerActual`, the receiver relay debt, the transformer actively-bridging burn gate). Never bypass the world-load sweep + tail settle when adding a new billing path; the APC's ledger stays unsettled (section 3.9).
- **The wireless pair's health and lockout state is pair-wise.** The receiver never yields its own seg description, so anything keyed by seg RefId (caches, lockouts, health) anchors on the transmitter; code touching the receiver half must go through `PartnerRefId` / the roster's partner reference, and a cycle-faulted receiver must lock the pair.
- **One boundary read per device per tick, in `GridSnapshot.Build` and nowhere else.** Every consumer of a device's demand or output reads the snapshot rows. Adding a second live `GetUsedPower` / `GetGeneratedPower` call anywhere in the pipeline reintroduces exactly the mid-tick tear the snapshot exists to kill (a fabricator's print-start spike landing between two reads). The segmenter surfaces are safe to sample during the build because the presentation shims serve the PREVIOUS tick's published totals, which is what vanilla OBSERVE saw.
- **Never read `PowerDeviceList`.** The lazy getter rebuilds the list in place with no lock, so reading it from the worker races the rebuild. The snapshot derives power membership from `lock(DeviceList)` plus the same predicate the vanilla rebuild uses (`device.PowerCables[i].CableNetwork == net`); any new topology consumer must do the same or consume the snapshot rows.
- **`MainThreadDebitQueue` has strict thread ownership.** `Post` / `Reconcile` / `PendingFor` / `Clear` are power-worker-only; `DrainOnMain` is main-thread-only. Never subtract `_powerUsedDuringTick` on the worker for the main-queue classes: the main thread's `+=` is a non-atomic load-add-store whose blind store can land after a successful worker CAS and resurrect already-billed joules (the rejected CAS design, POWER.md section 0 decision 26).
- **Segmenter control state goes through `SegControlSnapshot`.** The presentation shims consult the snapshot's (OnOff, Error) instead of live fields, so a player toggle landing mid-tick takes effect next tick, coherently with the grant. A new segmenter-surface patch that reads `__instance.OnOff` live would tear against the allocator's billing.
- **A multi-target patch class needs a bare class-level `[HarmonyPatch]` marker.** A class whose methods each carry their own `[HarmonyPatch(target)]` is silently skipped by `PatchAll` without the empty class-level attribute -- no error, the postfixes just never attach (`BurnReasonPatches` shipped dark this way until a ScenarioRunner probe caught it).
- **Runtime recipe overrides must ride `WorldManager.OnGameDataLoaded`.** `Plugin.OnPrefabsLoaded` fires before the game reads any mod's `GameData/` folder, so a recipe written there is silently clobbered by the overlay pass (including this mod's own `cable-recipes.xml`). Subscribe to the event instead; it fires after every overlay and the per-fabricator `GenerateRecipieList` calls.

## 8. Design decisions

The decision log spans three named phases (the 2026-05-12 first build pass, the 2026-05-13 round-2 follow-up that resolved every stubbed / deferred item, and the 2026-05-13 round-3 device-rejection -> reactive-cable-burn pivot) plus dated entries, newest first, through the 2026-07-12 atomic snapshot pipeline rebuild. Entries are append-only; supersedes are explicit parentheticals on the superseded entry.

### D1 -- D13 (2026-05-12)

- **D1**: Pure-patch mod, no new prefabs. Trims dependencies (no LibConstruct, no asset bundle), simplifies game-update maintenance.
- **D2**: Derive from Sukasa's Re-Volt (MIT) for the simulation half rather than reimplement from scratch. Attribution per section 11.
- **D3**: All config is server-authoritative; `Server - *` sections only.
- **D4**: Code name `PowerGridPlus`, display name `Power Grid Plus`.
- **D5**: NEW-3 research spike done; verdict was build-time rejection via `Cable.CanConstruct` postfix + reactive burn-on-join in `OnRegistered` postfix.
- **D6**: Three-tier voltage gating -- `normal`, `heavy`, `superHeavy` cables mutually incompatible, bridged only by transformers.
- **D7**: Super-heavy-cable cost multiplier default 2.0; NEW-1 (no burn limit) applies only to `superHeavy`.
- **D8**: Tier-by-draw, "lowest cable tier rated for peak draw / output". Stationary batteries on `heavy`; the >5 kW machines (Advanced Furnace, Arc Furnace, Carbon Sequester 45 kW/unit, Furnace, Centrifuge, Recycler, Ice Crusher, Hydraulic Pipe Bender, electric Deep Miner) on `heavy`+.
- **D9 (SUPERSEDED in round 2 by the universal cable-tier rule)**: per-prefab transformer-tier mapping (Small = `heavy<->normal`, Medium = `heavy<->heavy`, Large = `superHeavy<->heavy`).
- **D10 (SUPERSEDED in round 2 by the universal cable-tier rule)**: bidirectional transformers re-implementing `GetGeneratedPower` / `GetUsedPower` / `ReceivePower` symmetrically.
- **D11**: enforcement = build-time rejection (primary UX) + reactive burn-on-join (authoritative backstop + save migration). Burn-on-join algorithm: while a `CableNetwork` contains more than one `CableType`, pick a lowest-tier cable that is grid-adjacent to a higher-tier cable (the boundary cable) and `Break()` it. Fuses are never substituted: a tier mismatch is a topology violation, not over-current.
- **D12**: NEW-2 multiplies only the coil-crafting XML recipe; nothing touches per-cable `BuildStates[0].Tool.EntryQuantity`. Insulated cable variants count as their base `CableType`. In-game tier names stay "Normal / Heavy / Super-Heavy cable"; rejection message leans on the lore ("Cannot connect: different transmission voltage -- use a transformer").
- **D13**: all electrical generators are `heavy`-cable-only (overriding the per-output tier-by-draw rule for generators specifically). Solar Panels, the small Wind Turbine, Large Wind Turbine, Stirling Engine, Solid / Gas Fuel Generators, Turbine Generator, RTG -- all `heavy`. The build-time `Device.CanConstruct` rejection (then planned, now superseded by reactive burn per round-3 below) was the enforcement vehicle.

### Round 2 (2026-05-13): universal cable-tier rule

The user's round-2 instruction simplified the design: drop D9 / D10's per-prefab transformer tier mapping in favour of the universal rule "a `CableNetwork` must be all one tier; transformers and APCs have separate input / output networks and are therefore tier-exempt". This kept the spirit of D6 (the three tiers are separate voltages) but removed the need to distinguish transformer prefabs at all. `VoltageTier.IsTierExempt` was added; `Transformer`, `AreaPowerControl`, `WirelessPower`, and `PowerTransmitterOmni` are exempt. The "ordinary device on heavy / super-heavy = hard reject + no power if it slips through" rule was implemented at the tick level (`PowerGridTick.CalculateState_New` zeroes a device's used and generated power on the wrong tier) but the build-time hard reject was deferred (resolved in round 3). (Superseded in mechanism by the 2026-07-12 atomic pipeline: the `PowerGridTick` subclass is long deleted, and tier enforcement now runs in the PROTECT phase against the grid snapshot plus the main-thread topology recheck; the universal one-tier rule itself stands.)

### Round 3 (2026-05-13): build-time-device-rejection -> reactive cable-burn

The remaining round-2 gap was the build-time cursor reject for misplaced devices. On reflection the user preferred to NOT gate device placement at all and instead burn the cable next to the misplaced device, symmetric with the cable-tier rule. Done:

- `PowerGridTick.CalculateState_New` no longer zeroes a misplaced device's power. Instead it records the first misplaced device of the tick (`_misplacedDeviceForBurn`); `ApplyState_New` burns the cable adjacent to it via `VoltageTier.BurnCableForMisplacedDevice`. The device itself is never destroyed.
- Both tier-burns moved to `ApplyState_New`, gated on `powerFlow > 0`. An idle / off network destroys nothing, even if mixed-tier or with a misplaced device. As soon as actual power flows, the burn fires. Mixed-tier burns take precedence over device-tier burns (root cause first).
- The previously-removed `Cable_OnRegistered_Postfix` was confirmed unneeded. Cable registration triggers `DirtyPowerAndDataDeviceLists` -> `IsDirty = true` -> `Initialize_New` re-detects mixed-tier on the next tick. One code path covers fresh placement and loaded saves.
- `Cable_CanConstruct_Postfix` extended with a second pass over `Cable.ConnectedDevices()` so that placing a cable next to an existing device on a tier the device doesn't accept is rejected at the cursor.
- Burn-reason tooltips wired (see Architecture). Four reason categories: overload, wrong voltage (mixed-tier bridge), wrong voltage (adjacent misplaced device), and an untagged fallback.

Net result: build-time gating is cable-side only; device placement is never blocked. The "you put it on the wrong cable" feedback is the cable burning, with a tooltip on the wreckage explaining why. (Superseded in mechanism by the 2026-07-12 atomic pipeline: the `CalculateState_New` / `ApplyState_New` methods named here died with `PowerGridTick`; detection lives in `VoltageTierEnforcer` and the burns fire from the PROTECT phase and the write-back. The reactive cable-side-only policy itself stands.)

### 2026-07-13: Powered coupling revert, IC10 Power override, delivery shim

Three coupled changes on top of the same-day settings rework (POWER.md section 0 decision 28 carries the full rationale; the vanilla-touchpoint ledger is POWER.md section 2.1):

- **Powered follows the vanilla on/off coupling again.** The client session found the decoupling failed visually: vanilla stores the flag, the animator state, and the IC10 value in one place, and info screens plus several panel gates key on Powered alone, so a switched-off device kept a lit screen, a green button glow, and raised third-party force fields. The ownership sweep's expectation becomes verdict-LIVE AND structure complete AND the snapshot row's OnOff, so every Powered-keyed visual is vanilla-correct by construction; the sweep stays the single Powered writer (AssessPower suppressed), and a toggle edge lands when the next tick's snapshot carries the new OnOff (up to half a second), the deliberate race-free single-writer trade over a second mid-tick boundary read.
- **The IC10 Power read serves net liveness on swept plain devices** (`Patches/PowerLogicReadPatches.cs`): 1 iff the device's power network is verdict-LIVE, so scripts keep reading the grid signal they saw under the decoupled build; read On for the switch. Segmenters, exempt classes, emergency lights, quarantined, cable-less, and incomplete devices keep the vanilla `Powered ? 1 : 0`.
- **The plain-consumer delivery shim** restores the vanilla consumer-settle side effects for exactly five classes whose gameplay lives only inside `ReceivePower` (dead since the 2026-07-12 rebuild): `PowerTransmitterOmni` (wireless-battery charging), `SuitStorage` (suit recharge), `BatteryCellCharger` (slotted cells), `Bench` (appliances), `WallLightBattery` (cell top-up plus the grid-fed latch the emergency-light feature reads). A write-back step between storage settlement and the accumulator drains calls `device.ReceivePower(net, granted demand)` on verdict-LIVE nets, per-device try/catch; the fabricator family (debit-queue-owned accumulator) and stores / segmenters (write-back-owned ledgers) are excluded on purpose. New `Server - Compatibility` / `Extra Delivery Devices` setting (the 31st entry) enrolls modded devices; a load-time `ReceivePowerOverrideCensus` names candidates in the log.

### 2026-07-13: settings rework

The config surface was cut to 30 entries (31 since the delivery shim's `Extra Delivery Devices` landed later the same day; section 9 has the full table). Three groups of change:

- **Removed, behavior now always on**: `Enable Battery Limits`, `Enable Transformer Shedding`, `Enable Transformer Overload Protection`, `Enable Rocket Umbilical Limits`, `Enable Conservation Check`, `Decouple Powered From On Off` (Powered is always decoupled from the device's own on/off switch; the decoupling itself was reverted later the same day, see the entry above), and the five per-family `Enable * Logic Passthrough` masters (passthrough support is always on; a device's own `LogicPassthroughMode` is the only runtime gate). The `Server - Diagnostics` and `Server - Powered Presentation` groups are gone with their last settings.
- **Added: six per-kind Passthrough Default settings** (Battery, Small Transformer, Other Transformer, APC, Power Transmitter, Umbilical; default true except Other Transformer false). Each gives the `LogicPassthroughMode` a device starts with while its mode was never explicitly set (newly built, or an existing save running the mod for the first time); a stored per-device mode wins once set, and the APC (no logic port) is config-seeded only. `PassthroughModeStore.GetDefaultMode` reads them through the new `PassthroughDefaultsSync`.
- **Changed: `Battery Charge Efficiency` (default now 1.5) is a cost multiplier**: grid energy drawn per unit stored (`stored = delivered / value`; values below 1.0 treated as 1.0; post-loss trickles below 500 W stored in full). The default stores two thirds of the draw; the loss is destroyed for now, with a heat conversion planned.

Multiplayer wire change: the join suffix carries the six passthrough defaults and no longer carries shed or overload toggles; `PassthroughSettingsSync`, `ShedSettingsSync`, and `OverloadSettingsSync` are deleted in favour of `PassthroughDefaultsSync`.

### 2026-07-12: atomic snapshot pipeline (greenfield rebuild)

The power simulation was rebuilt greenfield around a mod-owned data plane (POWER.md section 0 decisions 22-26: full-strict isolation, always-on hardcoding, the B + D1 redesign, the race-free mandate). The decisions:

- **One prefix replaces `ElectricityManager.ElectricityTick`; no vanilla fallback.** The vanilla per-network `PowerTick` trio (`Initialise` / `CalculateState` / `ApplyState`) is never called on any path. The OBSERVE / ENFORCE phase names die with it; the pipeline is HOUSEKEEPING, SNAPSHOT, PROTECT, ALLOCATE, WRITE-BACK, the tick tail, then the vanilla device-tick and IC10 copies.
- **Single boundary read.** `Core/GridSnapshot.Build` samples every device's `GetUsedPower` / `GetGeneratedPower` exactly once per tick, with topology from `lock(DeviceList)` plus the vanilla power-port predicate (the racy `PowerDeviceList` lazy getter is never read). PROTECT and GATHER are snapshot-derived: the detectors and the allocator consume the rows, never live device state, and newly faulted producers are zeroed in the table (`ZeroFaultedProducers`) so the allocator solves the corrected grid same-tick. The read-once latch army of the previous architecture (consumer demand latches, solar / wind output latches, the control-state latches) is deleted; tick-stability is a property of the snapshot, not of per-class patches.
- **Ledger-style consumer accumulator drains (decision 26, user decision).** The `_powerUsedDuringTick` debit for the main-clock classes (SimpleFabricatorBase, Fabricator, ArcFurnace, IceCrusher) posts through one batched main-thread queue per tick paired with a worker-owned pending ledger (`effective accumulator = max(0, Volatile.Read(field) - pending)`); worker-owned classes (DroidSleeper, WallCooler, AdvancedComposter) debit synchronously at write-back. A worker-side CAS was evaluated and rejected: the main thread's non-atomic `+=` can blind-store over a successful CAS and double-bill. This is the race-free mandate (decision 25) applied: every mod-owned write is race-free by construction, "a smaller race than vanilla" is not accepted.
- **Full-strict producer isolation, always on.** The violator set is presence-based: any device on a producer's net that is neither a producer nor a `Transformer` faults the producers, idle consumers, batteries, APCs, dishes, and umbilicals included; a transformer on the net exempts nothing. Unrecognised producer-like devices (supplying power, not in the class list, not a segmenter) fall back to burning the cable adjacent to a foreign device.
- **Always-on hardcoding (decision 23).** Device Powered ownership, the transformer exploit mitigation, and the APC power fix lose their master toggles and become unconditional; only `Decouple Powered From On Off` remains in the Powered Presentation group. (The shed and overload masters and `Enable Battery Limits` survive as settings.)
- **Leaf-first shedding via hop protection.** A bridge whose output net still feeds an active downstream segmenter is protected from shed selection exactly like a step-up (`FeedsActiveSeg`, riding the same flag): it takes a partial grant and forwards the deficit instead of being decapitated, so sheds escalate leaf to trunk and a supply-chain trunk never faults out from under its subtree. Only leaf bridges with unmet rigid demand shed, lowest Priority first (`SelectShedVictims`).
- **`Core/WriteBack` owns all mutation.** Net load fields, the deterministic lowest-rated fuse blow, the 20-tick generator-overflow cable burn, storage settlement with credit == grant by construction (battery efficiency + trickle floor applied at credit; APC cell re-fetched at settlement; umbilical Last* mirrors via reflection), and the accumulator drains. The patched device power surfaces deliver nothing; they are presentation shims over the allocator's published totals.
- **DEAD nets freeze work.** A net that is not verdict-LIVE (`NetLiveness`, computed from the converged solve with a 120-tick DEAD_UNMET hold) settles no energy and drains no accumulators: unfunded work freezes exactly and is billed in full on revival. Powered follows the verdict, never per-device demand-met, so a device can no longer power-cycle on its own demand spike.
- **Fault sync stays per-tick full snapshots**, now with a join handshake: the join suffix ships all four registries' remaining-ticks maps so a joiner lands mid-lockout with correct flash and countdown.

### 2026-07-02: power rearchitecture (unified flow classes, segment adapters, billing ownership, ledger adoption)

Three staged changes landed together, motivated by the three-authority analysis (the vanilla per-network relaxation, this mod's allocator, and PowerTransmitterPlus's wireless billing all computing the same watts through two narrow shared channels, the `GetGeneratedPower` advertise and the `_powerProvided` ledger) and root-caused from a live fault-free deadlock on the Luna save (two 25 kW chargers idling forever). Dedi-verified per stage (batteries charge across seven wireless links to full; conservation checker zero violations; handshake live on both sides; census empty; tick-identical charge trajectory before and after the adapter refactor).

- **Stage 1 (commit e9ef3c1f): storage charge is a first-class Soft flow.** The single-pass surplus walk, `GrantThrough`, `SoftReqTotal`, and the dead `Net.ElasticDelivered` are deleted. Soft rides the same backward/forward sweep as rigid with a proportional split (killing the surplus walk's double-count by construction) and is granted from the firm residual only; faults stay rigid-only, so excess charge desire clamps silently instead of shedding or overloading anything. The cache tail publishes `TotalThrough` / `TotalPull` for EVERY routed seg kind, which is the actual deadlock fix. `ConservationChecker` added as a permanent in-mod invariant harness (`Server - Diagnostics`). Four documented deviations from the design sketch: the quiescent draw rides the soft pull when the rigid side is idle; the backward soft gate is `IsActive` (a shed contributor's charge desire must not size its suppliers, or delivered soft strands); the weakest-cable headroom cap applies to the per-net soft pool; and the APC also gets a `TransformerSupplyCache` entry so every routed kind publishes the same surface.
- **Stage 2b (commit 2a00d674): segment adapter contract + interop switch.** Bridge descriptions move behind `ISegAdapter` / `SegSpec` (`SegAdapters.cs`), deliberately preserving the exact arithmetic of the open-coded predecessors so allocator numbers stay bit-identical across the refactor. The umbilical is formalized as Buffered (store-and-forward; the 0.2.6403 bidirectional phase-2 crossing stays outside the allocator). `PowerTransmitterPlusInterop` grew the ModApi tier with the one-shot `ClaimBillingOwnership("net.powergridplus")` handshake (PowerTransmitterPlus 1.9.0+ stands its native wireless debt billing down while the claim is held); the legacy 1.8.0 reflection chain is intact underneath, and absent-PowerTransmitterPlus falls back to the vanilla model. `UnknownBridgeCensus` reports unmodelled third-party bridges instead of silently mis-modelling them.
- **Stage 3 (uncommitted at time of writing): Powered presentation + ledger adoption.** Healthy idle segmenters present Powered=True (AllowSetPower postfix gate + ENFORCE-tail true-edge reconcile via the healthy-set snapshot); the vanilla `_powerProvided` ledger is zeroed on world load and clamped per tick to `[0, 2 x bill]` (APC exempt: its positive ledger is the vanilla cell-discharge carrier). Kills the two vanilla-semantics leaks: the "idle charger reads unpowered" diagnostic trap and the saved negative wireless credit class (the -176,226 free-energy credit on Luna transmitter 464520).
- **Behaviour changes accepted knowingly**: soft flows now traverse intermediate networks for real, so hover / IC10 network readings include charge flow and fuse / cable ratings see the real load (the `CableMax` clamps keep granted flows within tier ratings); storage behind a high-priority transformer charges before storage behind a low-priority one (soft follows the rigid priority tiers instead of the old priority-blind walk).

(Superseded in part by the 2026-07-12 atomic pipeline: the vanilla `PowerTick` trio described as running inside the atomic phases no longer runs at all, so the `AllowSetPower` false-edge gate became the both-edge roster reconcile, the `[0, 2 x bill]` ledger clamp became the exact per-tick settle, and the vanilla-delivery path behind the patched surfaces is gone. The unified flow classes, the complete per-segment presentation, the adapter contract, the billing-ownership handshake, and the ledger sweep survive on top of the snapshot / write-back data plane.)

### 2026-06-23: data-only ports are tier-exempt (carve-out made explicit) + a load-regression fix

Two changes, both headless-verified on the dedi (ScenarioRunner `pgp-dataport-tier-diag` against `Luna_pgp_rocket`).

**Data-only ports accept any cable tier.** A device's exclusive data-only port (`NetworkType.Data`, no Power bit) must be invisible to the voltage-tier rules; only Power and power+data (`PowerAndData`) ports follow them. This was already true in practice: the game builds `CableNetwork.PowerDeviceList` from `Device.PowerCables` (power-bit cables only), so a device whose data port is on its own network is never in that network's `PowerDeviceList`, and the reactive Misplaced-burn (`DetectViolation` -> `BurnCableForMisplacedDevice`) iterates exactly that list. The cursor path likewise skipped data-only ports via `CursorAttachesToPowerPortOf` (added 2026-05-18). This session made the invariant explicit and regression-proof rather than incidental:

- `VoltageTier.ConnectionCarriesPower(Connection)` is the single predicate for "does this connection carry the Power bit"; the cursor's `CursorAttachesToPowerPortOf` now calls it.
- `VoltageTier.ReachesNetworkViaPowerPort(Device, CableNetwork)` is true iff one of the device's power cables sits on that network. `VoltageTierEnforcer.DetectViolation` now guards the Misplaced classification with it, so a device can never be tier-judged on a network it touches only through a data port, even if a future game/mod change leaked a data-only device into `PowerDeviceList`.

Verified headless: `runtimeInvariant=PASS` (realBugShape=0 over 402 live separate-data-port devices), `cursorCarveOut=PASS` (dataLeak=0 over 72 distinct data ports), across normal/heavy/superHeavy data networks; the 8 genuine power-tier would-burn networks stayed flagged (power and power+data enforcement unchanged). A separate data network may be any tier; a data cable that physically merges into a power network still follows the no-mixing rule (same tier, or bridge with a transformer), as intended. The on-screen cursor ghost color is the only client-side residue (a headless dedi cannot render the placement preview).

**csproj-migration load regression.** The 2026-06-22 SDK-style migration (`f5e67d37`) replaced the explicit `<Compile>` list with auto-glob, which pulled in `Patches/AreaPowerControlPassthroughLogicPatches.cs`, a dead file the old list deliberately excluded. Its `[HarmonyPatch(typeof(AreaPowerControl), nameof(AreaPowerControl.CanLogicWrite))]` cannot resolve (APC inherits `CanLogicWrite` from `Device`; it does not declare it), so `Harmony.PatchAll()` threw and the whole mod loaded with no patches applied. The file was superseded by `InheritedLogicablePassthroughLogicPatches` (which patches the base `Device` logic methods APC inherits), so it was deleted; the mod loads clean again (`patches applied`, no fatal). Lesson for the glob-all build: a dead `.cs` left on disk is a landmine, delete it rather than leave it excluded.

**superHeavy producers stay player error (decided 2026-06-23).** The same probe (PART-C) flagged seven superHeavy solar-collector networks (around 33 members each: solar panels, nuclear batteries, sensors) whose members would burn once the network carries power, because `IsAllowedOnTier` keeps generators, stationary batteries, and umbilicals on `heavy` exactly. Decided this is not a bug: placing a producer on a superHeavy backbone is incorrect wiring, and the heavy-only rule stands. No code change.

### 2026-06-21: rocket-family power parity (transformer tier, battery caps, umbilical heavy + logic passthrough)

Five changes resolving a friend's rocket-playtest report; the goal was to make rocket-internal power devices behave like their station equivalents and to make the power umbilical act like a real (disconnectable) wire.

- **Rocket transformer joins the tier map.** `StructureRocketTransformerSmall` was absent from `VoltageTier.GetTransformerTierMap`, so no tier-pair was enforced on it. Added it as {heavy, normal}, identical to `StructureTransformerSmall`; it now follows the same bidirectional pair rule and actively-bridging burn. (Its passthrough default-1 was already added 2026-06-19.)
- **Rocket batteries get the per-prefab rate cap.** `StationaryBatteryPatches.GetChargeCap` / `GetDischargeCap` switch on PrefabName, and the rocket batteries (`StructureBatteryMedium`, `StructureBatterySmall`) fell through to the uncapped `Infinity` default -- the only `Battery`-class devices without a rate cap. Added cases plus four `Server - Batteries` settings (`RocketBatteryMedium*` 5000 / 10000, `RocketBatterySmall*` 2500 / 5000). Everything else (heavy-cable tier, passthrough default 1, elastic shares, charge efficiency) already applied via the `Battery` class. The cap defaults are throughput estimates, tunable in-game.
- **Power umbilicals are heavy-cable-only.** `VoltageTier.IsAllowedOnTier` had no umbilical case, so the umbilical fell to the ordinary-device "normal cable only" branch and the mod burned a heavy cable wired to it -- the exact cause of the "needs to accept Heavy Cable" report. Added `IsRocketPowerUmbilical` (Male / Female) to the heavy-only branch alongside generators and stationary batteries.
- **Docked umbilical pair = a logic bridge.** Vanilla carries no logic across the umbilical coupling (a battery-buffer transfer with a null `CableNetwork`; see `Research/GameClasses/RocketPowerUmbilical.md`). Modeled the pair on the wireless dish-link: `PassthroughTopology` now treats both umbilical halves as a bridge type, gated on a non-null `_partnerUmbilical` (read by reflection -- `IUmbilical` has no public partner getter) so it bridges only while docked. `GetBridgeNetworks` yields own + partner power / data networks; a new `Server - Rocket Umbilical / Enable Umbilical Logic Passthrough` toggle (default on) joins the four existing passthrough toggles in `PassthroughSettingsSync` and the join suffix (now five booleans). Both halves expose a writable `LogicPassthroughMode` (default 1): the Female rides the base-`Device` glue (`InheritedLogicablePassthroughLogicPatches.IsBridge` extended), the Male needs a direct `CanLogicRead` patch in the new `UmbilicalPassthroughPatches` (it overrides that method). Per the "two things, one mode" requirement, a write to one half mirrors to the docked partner (`SetLogicValuePatch`). `UmbilicalPassthroughPatches` also refreshes the merged device lists on `SetPartner` (dock / undock), mirroring `DishLinkPatches`. Per-device mode persists via the existing side-car (umbilical cases added to `PassthroughSaveLoadPatches`).

Multiplayer note: the partner reference is set per-peer by geometry (`FindAndSetOtherUmbilical`), so each peer should bridge locally without a replicated back-reference; unconfirmed in MP (see `PLAYTEST.md`). If a client fails to bridge, add a client-side partner mirror like the dish back-reference mirror in `DishLinkPatches`.

### 2026-05-15: bidirectional transformer tier-pair + actively-bridging burn gate

The per-variant transformer rule lands but in two refined forms vs the original D9/D10 design:

- **Bidirectional unordered pair.** Each variant defines an unordered set `{tierA, tierB}` that the two cable ports must form together; direction does not matter. A Small is heavy/normal in either order; a Large is superHeavy/heavy in either order; a Medium is heavy/heavy. This covers both step-down (load-side wiring: heavy in, normal out) and step-up (generation-side wiring: normal in, heavy out) without distinguishing prefab-orientation variants. Reasoning: the original asymmetric rule only considered power-usage direction and didn't fit generators feeding into a heavy backbone via a Small transformer (which is a normal-side source pumping into a heavy-side network).
- **Actively-bridging burn gate.** The reactive cable burn for a transformer tier violation no longer fires whenever the local network has power flow. The stricter condition is `Transformer.OnOff == true` AND `Transformer._powerProvided > 0` (the previous tick's throughput was positive, i.e. the transformer was actually bridging power on both sides). When that condition is met AND the pair is violated, BOTH adjacent cables burn. Reasoning: transformers are expensive to build; punishing a misconfigured-but-off transformer feels wrong from a player-experience standpoint. The burn fires only when the player has actually committed to using the bad bridge.
- **Cable cursor reject scope confirmed.** Only cables are cursor-rejected, never transformers / devices. Cursor logic for the transformer-pair rule: cable's tier must be in the pair; for asymmetric variants with exactly one side wired, the new cable's tier must differ from that side's tier. Mirror of the APC rule (which requires same tier on both sides). Both-sides-wired and neither-side-wired cases handled by the cable-to-cable network-tier check upstream.

- `Transformer` gets a writable `LogicPassthroughMode` logic slot (`StationeersPlus.Shared.LogicTypeNumbers.LogicPassthroughMode`, ushort 6577). Per-Transformer override stored in `PassthroughModeStore`; defaults to 1 for `StructureTransformerSmall` + `StructureTransformerSmallReversed` + `StructureRocketTransformerSmall`, 0 for every other transformer prefab. Persists across save / load via an XML side-car in the save ZIP.
- Power Grid Plus joins the central `Patterns/Logic/` catalogue: `Patterns/Logic/LogicTypeNumbers.cs` is linked into the csproj; the integer 6577 lives there, not in `LogicTypeRegistry.cs`. The catalogue's reservation rules and the table of every SixFive7 LogicType assignment live in `Patterns/Logic/README.md`.
- Direction A vs B vs C for the Transformer-logic-transparency feature was decided: option B (per-mod patch on `CableNetwork.RefreshPowerAndDataDeviceLists`, no need to implement `ITransmitDataNetworkDevices` / `IReceiveDataNetworkDevices`). Symmetric: the bridging device acts as both transmitter and receiver against itself. The transformer's own logic ports (Setting, PowerActual) are visible from both sides because the transformer sits in BOTH networks' `DeviceList` already (one cable connection per side).

### 2026-05-18: passthrough extended to Battery / PowerTransmitter / PowerReceiver, and multiplayer merge determinism

- **Passthrough generalised from Transformer + APC to five device kinds.** `Battery`, `PowerTransmitter`, and `PowerReceiver` join the bridge (commit 33fc10b). A battery bridges its own `InputNetwork` <-> `OutputNetwork` (the two cable sides of a station battery); a linked dish pair bridges `tx.InputNetwork` <-> `rx.OutputNetwork` across the wireless link. All three default to `LogicPassthroughMode = 1`. `PassthroughModeStore` was generalised from keying on `Transformer` to keying on any `Thing.ReferenceId`, and the side-car save / load now covers all four mode-bearing types; the on-disk schema is unchanged ((`ReferenceId`, `Mode`) pairs), so a v0.1.0 transformer-only save still loads. Two new server toggles, both default on: `Server - Batteries / Enable Battery Logic Passthrough` and `Server - Power Transmitters / Enable Power Transmitter Logic Passthrough`. `AreaPowerControl` stays a flat toggle with no per-device mode.
- **Base-`Device` patch for the inherited case.** `Battery` / `PowerTransmitter` / `PowerReceiver` do not override the four `Logicable` methods, so the logic ports are wired by patching base `Device` with a runtime `IsBridge` guard (`InheritedLogicablePassthroughLogicPatches`). Attribute-patching the subclasses directly throws "Undefined target method" at `PatchAll` time and silently disables the rest of the batch; see section 7 and `Research/Patterns/HarmonyLogicableInheritedMethodTrap.md`. `Transformer` keeps its own direct patch because it does override the methods.
- **`SetLogicValue` is server-only.** Both the `Transformer` and base-`Device` `SetLogicValue` prefixes bail unless `NetworkManager.IsServer`, so a client tablet write routes through the host instead of mutating the client's local store. Verifying this did not break the tablet-routed write path is an open playtest item in `TODO.md`.
- **Deterministic network merge (commit 14946c5).** `CableNetwork.Merge` and `StructureNetwork.Merge` keep `list[0]` as the survivor, and server and client run the merge independently with no authoritative sync event, so divergent cable-graph iteration order makes the two sides keep different survivors and read different `ReferenceId`s for the same network forever. `MergeDeterminismPatches` sorts the merge-input list by `ReferenceId` first, making the survivor a pure function of server-allocated ids. Covers `WirelessNetwork` (via `CableNetwork`) and rocket / robotic-arm / landing-pad networks (via `StructureNetwork`). See `Research/GameClasses/CableNetwork.md` and `Research/Patterns/MultiplayerStateMutation.md`.

### 2026-05-21: logic passthrough made transitive (multi-hop, cycle-safe)

The 2026-05-18 merge was single-hop: each bridge unified only its two immediate networks, so a logic reader more than one bridge from its target could not see it. Repro on the Lunar save: an IC10 housing on cable network 407130 runs `sb ComputerBigScreen On` against a screen on 407119, two transformers away (407130 -> small transformer 397999 -> spine 398122 -> small transformer 401261 -> 407119); the screen was never in the housing's data device list, so the batch write never reached it.

Fix: the merge now walks the TRANSITIVE closure of the bridge graph.

- `Patches/PassthroughTopology.cs` (new) holds the shared helpers. `IsEnabledBridge(device)` is the per-device bridge test (server toggle + per-device mode), so which devices bridge is unchanged. `GetBridgeNetworks(device)` yields every network a bridge device connects (power input, power output, dedicated `DataCableNetwork`, and a linked dish's far power+data networks). `GatherReachable(start)` is a stack BFS over the bridge graph with a `HashSet<CableNetwork>` visited set: for each enabled bridge on a network it folds in ALL of that device's other networks (all-port bridging), returns every reachable network except `start`, ordered by `ReferenceId` so the merge order is identical on every peer. The visited set makes a cyclic bridge graph terminate (each network folded in once).
- `Patches/LogicPassthroughPatches.cs` postfix merges every reachable network's `DeviceList` into the local `_dataDeviceList` via `GatherReachable`, instead of only the immediate neighbours.
- `Patches/CableNetworkPatches.cs` adds the correctness half: the `DirtyPowerAndDataDeviceLists` postfix now propagates the dirty across the whole component (re-entrancy-guarded `[ThreadStatic]` flag; gated on `GameManager.RunSimulation` to skip world-load / join churn). Vanilla dirties only the network that directly changed; without propagation a far network's change leaves dependent merged lists stale. It calls `DirtyPowerAndDataDeviceLists` (both flags) because `DataDeviceList.get` checks the power flag (the section 7 quirk).

Why approach 1 (per-network BFS + propagation) over a component-precompute or a global-generation cache: the merge runs only at structural-change cadence (the dirty-gated refresh, never per tick -- see `Research/GameClasses/CableNetwork.md` "Refresh cadence"), real components are small, and this keeps the existing per-network local-mutation model with no shared cross-network state (lowest multiplayer / threading risk). If a profiler ever flags a large hot component, promote `GatherReachable`'s union into a per-component shared cache.

Verified on the dedicated server with the Lunar save (2026-05-21, game 0.2.6228.27061) via a temporary merge log: network 407130 resolved a 22-network component (the whole solar tower, bridged by mode-1 transformers / batteries / dishes) with the screen 400892 present in its merged list; 65 components computed at load with no hang (cycle-safe on a real cyclic topology), world loaded in 15s.

Trade-offs: (1) propagation is server-gated, so a client's cross-network data list can be briefly stale after a far-network change until its own next refresh -- cosmetic, server (IC10) reads stay correct, self-heals. (2) Transitivity makes every mode-1-bridged network in a connected web mutually logic-transparent, so a batch write reaches all matching devices across the whole component (the entire tower), not just the nearest -- intended, but worth knowing on heavily-bridged bases.

### 2026-05-23: cable heat dissipation rejected as unworkable

A `Server - Cable Heat Dissipation` feature was considered (idea borrowed from the [Deadly Electricity](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3575959825) mod): each tick, convert a small fraction of a cable network's throughput into thermal energy deposited into the cable's local atmosphere, so wires get warm under load. Decompile research into Stationeers grid topology and cable placement showed the feature is unworkable in practice, and it was dropped before any code landed.

The blocking fact is the cable's coordinate resolution. A cable's `WorldGrid` resolves to a single 2 m large cell via `GridCenter()` on its `Transform.position`. There is no finer-grained atmosphere resolution; `AtmosphericsManager.AllWorldAtmospheresLookUp` is keyed by 2 m cells only. The placement system biases face-mounted cables (the body-side-bias documented for face-mounted structures in `Research/GameClasses/Cladding.md`) into the cell containing the supporting frame, so a cable visibly running along the room side of a wall still anchors to the wall's cell, not the room's.

The consequence is severe: every cable mounted on a fully-built frame -- whether buried in the cable channel or laid on a visible outer surface -- atmospherically belongs to the frame's sealed cell. That cell has no atmosphere entry at all (`GridController.CanContainAtmos` returns false; `RoomController.GetRoom` returns null; the only fallback is `PlanetaryAtmosphereSimulation.ReadOnlyGlobal`, which is the world climate and not safe to write to). The engine's own pattern for this case is `Atmosphere.CalculateThingEntropy` at decompile line 417511: `if (!CanContainAtmos) return Zero` -- no sink, no dissipation. The only configurations that would actually couple a cable to a real atmosphere are floor-laid cables in genuinely open cells and cables inside iron-frame-stage frames (build index 1, `HasPartialFrame = true`). In any normal mid-to-late-game base, near-zero cables qualify.

That makes the feature pointless. Under a "quiet drop when sealed" implementation it would silently no-op for almost every cable in play, failing the wires-get-warm intent. Under an "accumulate-and-burn when sealed" implementation it would blanket-punish the standard placement pattern of running cables inside walls and structures, which players cannot reasonably avoid. Either implementation is bad in a different way, and there is no third option the grid topology allows.

Dropped from consideration; no implementation work, no config surface, no settings group. The general-purpose heat-emission API research produced during this investigation stays useful for any future feature that emits heat from something with a real atmosphere reference (a device, a pipe, or a Thing on the atmospheric thing list); see `Research/GameSystems/HeatEmissionToAtmosphere.md`.

## 10. Pure-patch invariants

- **No new prefabs.** The mod's only added content is the `GameData/cable-recipes.xml` overlay. Every patch targets a vanilla type. Removing the DLL from a save preserves every existing thing in the world.
- **No save mutation.** Cable Watts caps are runtime-only (`CableMax`; the serialized `Cable.MaxVoltage` is never written), the `_powerProvided` ledgers are settled in memory, fault lockouts are transient (never serialized; wiped at load), and `Transformer.Setting` is initialised only for fresh constructions (saved values win on load). Removing the mod restores vanilla ratings and semantics with no residue.
- **Side-car XMLs are skipped by vanilla.** `pwrgridplus-passthrough.xml`, `pwrgridplus-priority.xml`, and `pwrgridplus-burnreason.xml` inside the save ZIP are unknown to the vanilla loader; uninstalling the mod and saving once rebuilds the ZIP without them. The per-device `LogicPassthroughMode` overrides, transformer priorities, and wreckage burn reasons are forgotten silently; the affected devices revert to vanilla behaviour.
- **Multiplayer is required.** `MOD.Networking.Required = true`. Clients without the matching DLL are rejected at join time. Re-Volt and MoreCables are refused at load on the local side too (`TryFindIncompatibleMod`): Re-Volt rewrites the same power tick, MoreCables adds cable tiers the voltage-tier policy cannot classify (see Appendix B for the compat table).

Reading guide:
- Columns A-D are the "power-system overhaul" space. Only Re-Volt populates all four; Power Grid Plus populates A/B/C and the recursive-network bit of D (no Load Center, no breakers); Deadly Electricity populates A. Nothing else touches this space, which is exactly why Re-Volt and Deadly Electricity are the only true competitors.
- Columns E-H are "balance / per-device tuning", mostly XML or value-patch mods that coexist with everything.
- Columns I-L are "new power content". The ones that matter for Power Grid Plus: MoreCables (col K, adds super-heavy / super-conductor tiers the three-tier gating must classify), CableTypeSwitcher and EL Switche (col K, create/convert cables at runtime, bypassing the build-time gate).
- Column M is logic / monitoring / QoL, all orthogonal.
- Column N is the Power-Grid-Plus-only features.
- For the per-feature detail of the A-D space (the 48 Re-Volt features), see Appendix A. For each mod's full feature bullets and the conflict / compat assessment, see Appendix B.
