- **Structural overload vs leaf-first deprioritization: decide the precedence (user question 2026-07-13).** `DetectStructuralOverload` (PowerAllocator) is desire-based, runs BEFORE the deprioritization pass, and is grow-only within the tick: a net whose rigid demand plus downstream rigid desires exceeds gen + elastic + its Setting-limited suppliers' combined EffectiveCapacity overloads those suppliers, and the in-code comment states the intent ("the overload commits even if a same-tick deprioritization in its subnetwork would have removed the condition; overload is the structural signal the player must act on"; precedence CYCLE > CURRENT-MISMATCH > CABLE-OVERLOADED > DEVICE-OVERLOADED > DEPRIORITIZED, POWER.md decision 3). Observed on the Luna base: parallel pair 511091/511092 (both Setting 50000, combined ~100 kW) trips OVERLOAD because the subtree's pre-deprioritization rigid desire exceeds the pair's rating, even though deprioritizing the subtree's lowest-priority leaves might have fit the residual under the cap. Alternative rule to consider: run the structural check on POST-deprioritization residual desire, so a subnet with deprioritization-eligible leaves degrades leaf-first and the trunk only overloads when even the post-deprioritization floor exceeds its rating (matches the "downstream subsystems are deprioritized first" intuition; the cost is that an undersized trunk stays hidden until deprioritization is exhausted). Note for the analysis: soft (charge) desire is correctly excluded from the structural sum already (`BackwardDesirePass` separates DesiredPull from SoftDesiredPull), so this is purely about rigid desire ordering.
